The mass of a hot-air balloon and its cargo (not including the air inside) is 200kg. The air outside is at 10.0°C and 101kPa. The volume of the balloon is 400m3. To what temperature must the air in the balloon be heated before the balloon will lift off? (Air density at 10.0°C is 1.25kg/m3.)

Formulas used in hot-air balloon solution

    ➤ Buoyant Force is equal to the weight of air displaced by the hot-air balloon $$\begin{aligned} \text{Buoyant Force} &=\text{Pressure}\times \text{Area} \\ \text{B} &= (\rho_{\text{cold}}- \rho_{\text{hot}}) gh\times A \\ &= (\rho_{\text{cold}}- \rho_{\text{hot}}) gV \end{aligned}$$
    ➤Gas law equation $$\begin{aligned} PV &= nRT \\ \frac{n}{V} &= \frac{P}{RT} \\ \frac{\text{m}_{air}}{V} &= \frac{\text{M}_{air}P}{RT} \\ \frac{\text{m}_{air}}{V} &= \frac{\text{const}}{T}\\ \text{density, ρ} &= \frac{\text{const}}{T}\\ \text{ρ} &∝ \frac{1}{T} \\ \text{ (Density of air, ρ}& ⇒ \text{ inversely proportional to temperature )}\\ \text{ρ}_{\text{hot}} &=\frac{\text{ρ}_{\text{cold}} T_{\text{cold}}}{T_{\text{hot}}} = \frac{\text{ρ}_{\text{c}} T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}}\end{aligned}$$
$$\begin{aligned} \textbf{B} &= \Big(\rho_{\text{c}}- \frac{\rho_{\text{c}} T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}}\Big) gV \\ \textbf{B} &= \rho_{\text{c}} \Big[ 1 - \frac{T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}} \Big]gV\end{aligned}$$
when Buoyant Force is greater than the Weight, the Hot-Air Balloon lifts. i.e $$\begin{aligned} \textbf{B} &= mg \\ \rho_{\text{c}} \Big[ 1 - \frac{T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}} \Big]gV &= mg\\ or, \Big[ 1 - \frac{T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}} \Big]&= \frac{m}{\rho_{\text{c}} V} \end{aligned}$$ Calculation:
Given:- density, ρ = 1.25 kg/m3; mass, m = 200 kg; volume, V = 400 m3; Tc= 10+273 = 283K $$\begin{aligned} 1 - \frac{T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}} &=\Big( \frac{200}{1.25 \times 400}\Big) \\ 1 - \frac{T_{\text{c}}}{T_{\text{h}}} &= \frac{1}{2.50} \\ 1 - \frac{283}{T_{\text{h}}} &= 0.4 \\ 0.6 &= \frac{283}{T_{\text{h}}} \\ T_{\text{h}} &= \frac{283}{0.6} \\ T_{\text{h}} &= 472K \\or, T_{\text{h}} &= 472 -273 = 199\text{°C}\end{aligned}$$
