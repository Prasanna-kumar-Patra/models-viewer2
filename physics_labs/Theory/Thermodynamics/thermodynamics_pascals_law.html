Pascal's Law

In view of the fact that the pressure in a fluid depends on depth and on the value of atmospheric pressure (P0), any increase in pressure at the surface must be transmitted to every other point in the fluid. This concept was first recognized by the French scientist Blaise Pascal (1623-1662) and is called Pascal's law.

    ➤ A change in the pressure applied to a fluid is transmitted undiminished to every point of the fluid and to the walls of the container.

Hydraulic Press

An important application of Pascal's law is the hydraulic press.A force of magnitude F1 is applied to a small piston of surface area A1. The pressure is transmitted through an incompressible liquid to a larger piston of surface area A2. Because the pressure must be the same on both sides,

P =
F1 / A1
=
F2 / A2
or, F2 =
F1 A2 / A1
    ➤ By designing a hydraulic press with appropriate areas A1 and A2, a large output force can be applied by means of a small input force.
    ➤ Hydraulic brakes, car lifts, hydraulic jacks, and forklifts all make use of this principle

Work Done
